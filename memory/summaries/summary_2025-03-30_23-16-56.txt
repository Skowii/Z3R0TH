CSS units cheat sheet includes various measurements for web design. Absolute pixel values are denoted by 'px', while relative units include: rem (root element's font-size), em (element's font-size), %, vw (viewport width, e.g., 1vw = 1% viewport width), vh (viewport height, e.g., 1vh = 1% viewport height), vmin (smaller dimension of viewport, e.g., 1vmin = min(1vh, 1vw)), vmax (larger dimension of viewport, e.g., vmax = max(1vh, 1vw)), ch (width of glyph "0" of the element's font), and traditional units like inches (in), picas (pc), and points (pt). Conversions between these units can also be found in this cheat sheet.
